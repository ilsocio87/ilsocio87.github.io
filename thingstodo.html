THINGS TO DO

If you have a day or two to spare, there’s plenty to see and do in the area.

AROUND DALHOUSIE

Rosslyn Chapel – This historic building has been used as a place of worship since 1446 and became world famous thanks to its role in Dan Brown’s novel, The Da Vinci Code and the film adaptation that followed.

Glenkinchie Distillery – learn more about Scotland’s national drink on a guided tour of this historic distillery including a whisky tasting.

EDINBURGH

There’s so much to see and do in Edinburgh that it’s hard to know where to start. You can’t go wrong with a visit to Edinburgh Castle, the National Museum of Scotland, the Palace of Holyroodhouse, The Edinburgh Dungeon or The Real Mary King’s Close.

If you want to stretch your legs and get a great view of the city, head to the top of Arthur’s Seat, an extinct volcano situated just off The Royal Mile.
Or you could just go to the pub…

Visit this website for more information.

FURTHER AFIELD

If you have a couple of days to spare, Scotland has so much history, tradition and stunning scenery to offer.

Venture further North to explore the Highlands, where you’ll be blown away by the beauty of the lochs and mountains, or take a trip to the Isle of Skye, famous for its distilleries and rugged landscape.

For anyone who likes a walk on the wild side, why not check out the place Simone once dubbed ‘the most dangerous city in the world’ – Glasgow!
